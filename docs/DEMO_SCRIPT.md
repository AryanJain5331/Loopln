# LoopIn five-minute demo script

Target length: 4 minutes 30 seconds. Keep the final 30 seconds as upload margin.

## 0:00-0:30 · Problem

> Students do not just need more links. They need someone who knows what is
> relevant to them right now. LoopIn is an AI senior friend that closes the
> information gap around internships, hackathons, tools, and learning updates.

Show the landing page.

## 0:30-1:10 · Profile intake

Enter a name, year, location, stack, goals, and interests.

> LoopIn starts with context: year of study, tech stack, goals, interests, and
> location. This is what generic feeds usually miss.

Click **Loop me in**.

## 1:10-1:55 · Reasoning pipeline

Let the five stages complete.

> The agent follows the pipeline from the synopsis: profile intake, contextual
> search, relevance reasoning, personalized synthesis, and action delivery. The
> trace shows why each step exists instead of hiding everything behind a spinner.

## 1:55-3:20 · Personalized cards

Scroll through the cards and open one deep dive.

> Each card is written like advice from a senior friend. It says what the
> opportunity is, why it matters for this student, and what action to take next.
> Sources stay attached so the student can verify deadlines and eligibility.

Open the official source link.

## 3:20-4:00 · Safety and equity

Show the safety section.

> LoopIn is careful with deadline-sensitive claims. It does not store accounts
> or sensitive student data in demo mode. The social goal is simple: opportunity
> should depend on potential, not on whether someone already has the right
> network.

## 4:00-4:30 · Architecture

Show `docs/architecture.svg`.

> The app has a local demo mode and a server-side Microsoft Foundry path. The
> live path uses Microsoft Agent Framework, Foundry IQ for the ranking rubric,
> and source retrieval before returning structured cards to the React UI.

End on the product name and repository URL.
